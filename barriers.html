Barriers to Adoption & The Role of Education, Policy, and Community

Common barriers include:

  • High initial investment: Equipment and infrastructure can be costly up front.
  • Limited technical capacity: Not all farmers have access to training or demonstration plots.
  • Short-term uncertainty: Benefits often accrue over several years, which can dissuade adoption.
  • Policy & incentive limitations: Programs may not fully support small-scale transitions.

How communities and institutions help

Extension programs (e.g., University of Kentucky Cooperative Extension) provide workshops, field days, and demonstration plots. USDA programs such as EQIP and CSP give financial and technical support. Farmer networks and conservation districts promote peer learning and locally adapted solutions.
