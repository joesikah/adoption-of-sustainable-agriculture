Key Sustainable Practices Gaining Ground in Kentucky

This section summarizes common practices being adopted and their benefits.

Practices covered

  • Conservation tillage and cover cropping
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM)
  • Efficient water and nutrient management
  • Rotational grazing and livestock integration
  • Diversified farming and agroforestry

Table 1. Common Sustainable Practices in Kentucky and Their Benefits

PracticeEnvironmental BenefitEconomic BenefitExample in Kentucky
Cover CroppingReduces erosion and increases soil organic matterLowers fertilizer costs and enhances yieldUsed widely in Bourbon & Fayette Counties
Conservation TillageProtects soil structure and reduces CO₂ emissionsCuts fuel and labor costsAdopted in western Kentucky corn and soybean fields
Rotational GrazingImproves pasture recovery and reduces runoffExtends grazing season and cuts feed costsKentucky Beef Network farms
Drip IrrigationConserves water and prevents nutrient leachingImproves crop quality and reduces pumping costsSpecialty crop growers in Central KY
Integrated Pest Management (IPM)Protects beneficial insects and pollinatorsReduces pesticide expensesStatewide through UK IPM Program

Sources: University of Kentucky Cooperative Extension Service (2024); USDA NRCS (2023); Kentucky Soil Health Coalition (2023).
