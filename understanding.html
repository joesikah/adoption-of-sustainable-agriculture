Understanding Sustainable Agricultural Practices

Sustainable agriculture integrates ecological, economic, and social principles so food production supports current needs without compromising future generations. Key goals are productivity, environmental protection, and social equity.

Key principles

  • Soil health improvement: Adding organic matter, crop rotation, and reducing tillage to maintain fertility, reduce erosion, and improve water retention.
  • Efficient resource use: Applying water and nutrients precisely using drip irrigation, moisture sensors, and variable-rate technology.
  • Biodiversity conservation: Diversified crops, hedgerows, and biological pest control to sustain pollinators and beneficial insects.
  • Economic viability: Practices that lower input costs, open market opportunities (e.g., organic), and support farmer livelihoods.

These principles are applied across Kentucky through locally adapted innovations and extension support.
